• info_outlineAbout Spatial Layers

    What are Spatial Layers
    Spatial Layers are sets of spatial features (points, lines and polygons) to be used for Spatial Reports.

    Where do these Spatial Layers come from?
    Spatial Layers are generated in a few different places in the Apps:
    codeCreated here in the Spatial Tools, using the +ADD MISC. LAYER button.
    codeCreated in the Development App as a Location for a Development Project.
    codeCreated programmatically, from other data.

    Examples
    labelCreated in the All or Misc. Layers interface: look for Layers where Type = Misc.
    labelDevelopment App: Look at any Development Project with a delineated footprint in the Location tab. This is a Spatial Layer. In the All Layers table, these layers are Type = Development.
    labelCreated programmatically: For example, the HMTK Species and HMTK Culture layers are custom translations of the ingested HMTK geodatabase tables (done in May 2016). Newer data added to HMTK Species and HMTK Culture records through the Heritage App (and admin pages) will not be in the Spatial layers until another translation is made.
